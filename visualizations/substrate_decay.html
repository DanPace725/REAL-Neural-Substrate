Substrate Decay — The Bistable Cliff

phase8/substrate.py · ConnectionSubstrate.tick()

Use it or lose it — fast

Every tick, every connection's support value decays. Above the bistable threshold (0.25) the decay is gentle — 0.025/tick. Below it, the decay rate is 2.5× faster. This creates a hard cliff: once a connection slips below the threshold, it collapses to zero in a handful of ticks unless immediately rescued. Maintenance pushes support back up; investment gives a larger boost. There's no gradual forgetting — just stable, borderline, or gone.

Velocity Tracking
What velocity means

The substrate tracks an exponential moving average of the change in support each tick (α=0.30). This velocity is injected into the observation as support_velocity_N, and the maintenance priority formula uses it directly: connections with negative velocity get a 4× boost in maintenance priority.

// velocity EMA, per tick
delta = slow[key] - _slow_prior[key]
velocity = α × delta + (1 - α) × velocity

// maintenance priority uses it
priority += max(0, -velocity) × 4.0
Active connection check

A connection is considered active if its support is at or above the bistable threshold. This is a hard binary check. Active connections are eligible for routing; inactive ones are not listed in available actions.

// is_active() in substrate.py
return slow[key] ≥ bistable_threshold

// → dead edge = completely inaccessible
There's no degraded routing. A connection below 0.25 isn't "weakly available" — it's gone from the action menu entirely. You can't route through it at all until it's reinvested above the threshold.
