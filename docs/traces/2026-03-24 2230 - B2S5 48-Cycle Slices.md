## Diagnosis

### Budget-shrinking heuristic degraded the run

The budget-shrinking rule fires when: `not improving AND uncertainty >= previous`.
With persistent ambiguity=1.0 and flat coherence_delta across all active slices,
the condition was met on most slices. Budget progression: 48 → 48 → 48 → 36 → 36
→ 36 → 27 → 20 → 15 → 11. By slice 10 the window was 11 cycles — too small to
accumulate meaningful bias signal.

Total cycles run: 48+48+48+36+36+36+27+20+15+11 = 325 out of 476 (68%). The
scenario was never fully covered. Exact matches dropped to 92 vs 126 in the 95-cycle
run because the later examples were never processed.

The budget-shrinking heuristic is working as designed for its original purpose
(converging to a minimal budget in well-behaved tasks) but is counterproductive
when ambiguity is structurally high and the goal is directed accuracy improvement
via repeated bias application.

### Context_1 progress was worse, not better

Context_1 accuracy across slices: 0.500 → 0.565 → 0.500 → 0.519 → 0.500 → 0.500
→ 0.571 → 0.542 → 0.500 → 0.409. Median ~0.5, never above 0.571. The per-context
bias is still applying, but with shrinking windows there is less substrate state to
read and the seeding signal is weaker. The 95-cycle run's context_1 trajectory
(0.523 → 0.508 → 0.493 → 0.516 → 0.577) was more consistent despite fewer slices.

### The 95-cycle result is the better operating point at B2S5

The 95-cycle run processed all 432 examples, matched baseline quality, and settled
(by exhaustion) with 88% cost reduction. The 48-cycle run processed only 75% of
examples and was worse on every metric. The conclusion is that per-slice budget
should be sized to complete the full scenario in 5 slices (or slightly fewer) so
the per-context bias accumulates across the full packet distribution.

### The settle-by-threshold at 0.7 remains out of reach at B2S5

Context_1's fundamental issue: `xor_mask_1010` and `rotate_left_1` have near-equal
hint weights (~0.546 each) at the source node. Alternative-transform seeding for
context_1 pushes `xor_mask_1010`, but the substrate must overcome an existing
preference shaped by the 302 context_1 packets that all defaulted to `identity` or
`rotate_left_1`. Five active slices of 95 cycles each produce improvement (0.48 →
0.58) but not enough to cross 0.7 within the 476-cycle scenario horizon.

## Working Read

The 95-cycle per-context bias run (`initial_cycle_budget=95, max_slices=10`) is the
correct operating point for B2S5. Reducing the budget to 48 to get more slices is
not beneficial because the budget-shrinking heuristic compounds the window reduction
and leaves the later part of the scenario unprocessed.

For the per-context bias to reach the 0.7 threshold on context_1, either:
- The task needs a longer scenario horizon (more examples) for the preference shift
  to accumulate, or
- The hint tie must be broken by a different mechanism (e.g., direct inspection of
  which transform is actually correct for context_1 via a cold-start seeding, rather
  than relying on history-derived hint weights that are identical for both transforms).

The latter suggests the slow layer may need access to the task's expected-transform
labels (or at least the direction of the accuracy gap: "context_1 is succeeding less
often than context_0 AND the current dominant transform for context_1 is
rotate_left_1") to seed the alternative more aggressively than the hint weights alone
support.

## Next Steps

1. Return to `initial_cycle_budget=95, max_slices=10` as the B2S5 operating point.
2. Consider whether the budget-shrinking rule should be suppressed or bounded when
   `accuracy_threshold > 0` and `weak_context_gap > 0` (i.e., directed-improvement
   mode). A simple guard: `if weak_context_gap > 0: next_slice_budget = current.slice_budget`
   would prevent the regulator from shrinking windows while the threshold is not yet met.
3. Test the 0.7 threshold on a task where the two contexts have more asymmetric hint
   weights — C3 family tasks may offer this.
